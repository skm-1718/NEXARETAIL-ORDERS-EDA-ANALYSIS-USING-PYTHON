## 📖 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the NexaRetail Orders dataset using Python. The objective is to understand customer purchasing behavior, sales performance, seasonal trends, return patterns, and operational performance while generating actionable business insights.

The analysis follows a structured data analytics workflow consisting of data cleaning, feature engineering, exploratory data analysis, visualization, statistical analysis, and business recommendations.

---

# 🎯 Objectives

- Understand the overall sales performance.
  
- Analyze customer purchasing patterns.
  
- Identify seasonal sales trends.
  
- Compare sales across store types and channels.
  
- Evaluate product return behavior.
  
- Discover business opportunities through data-driven insights.

# 📈 Key Business Insights

## 📌 1. Strong Seasonal Demand

Revenue peaked during **July (₹5.07 Million)** while **April** recorded the lowest revenue. This indicates significant mid-year seasonality that should be considered for inventory planning and marketing campaigns.

---

## 📌 2. Revenue is Dominated by Traditional Retail

- Kirana Stores generated **₹35.81 Million**
  
- Supermarkets generated **₹11.85 Million**

Similarly,

- Offline Sales generated **₹33.61 Million**
  
- Online Sales generated **₹14.04 Million**

The business currently relies heavily on offline retail channels, indicating an opportunity to expand digital sales.

---

## 📌 3. Product Return Analysis

The highest return rate was observed in:

- Atta (38.87%)

Lowest Return Rate:

- Beverages (36.08%)

Returns appear to be more closely related to product categories than sales channels.
